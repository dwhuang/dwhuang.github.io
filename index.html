About Me

I expect to graduate in August 2016, and I am actively looking for a job.
I am a PhD candidate working with Jim Reggia on neural-based machine learning. My dissertation research focuses on self-organizing map neural architectures based on limit cycle dynamics. I am also working on an ONR project studying effective imitation learning for robots.
I have a passion for programming.
I used to work on wireless mesh networks while I was a graduate student in National Taiwan University.

Software

SMILE is an open-source simulated 3D environment supporting studies in robot imitation learning, based on our virtual demonstrator hypothesis. See project page and publications for details.
